Frequently Asked Questions

What does this tool do?

It acts as a shortcut manager to quickly save and organize your favorite websites.

Is it free?

Yes, the extension is completely free to use.

Where is my data stored?

All data is stored locally on your device within your browser.

Can I add a custom image for a site?

Yes! When adding or editing a site, you can upload a custom icon image (PNG, JPG, SVG, or ICO, max 128KB).

How does visit tracking work?

Each time you open a saved site from the extension, its visit count increases. The most visited sites appear in the "Most Visited" section at the top.

How does Fuzzy Search work?

Fuzzy search lets you find sites even if you misspell or only remember part of the name. It uses bigram matching and sequential character analysis to find the best matches.

What are the keyboard shortcuts?

Ctrl+Shift+S (Cmd+Shift+S on Mac) to quick-save the current page. Ctrl+Shift+C (Cmd+Shift+C on Mac) to activate the Web Clipper on the current page.

How does the Web Clipper work?

The Web Clipper lets you select text or images on any webpage and save them as clips. You can also right-click selected text and choose "Clip Selected Text" from the context menu.

What are Quick Notes?

Quick Notes let you add a personal description or comment to any saved website. The note is displayed directly on the site card for easy reference.

What are Smart Tags?

Tags let you label sites with multiple keywords (e.g., "Free", "AI", "Design"). When adding a site, the system suggests tags based on your existing ones. You can filter by tags to quickly find what you need.

How does the Broken Link Checker work?

It automatically checks your saved sites every 6 hours for dead links. A red badge appears on the extension icon if broken links are found. You can also run manual checks from the Settings → Broken Links tab.
